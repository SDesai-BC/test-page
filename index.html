Case study

Imagine a professional association that represents CPAs — about 5,000 members, mostly working at small to mid-sized accounting firms or in solo practice. Members pay around $500 a year for things like networking events, an annual conference, continuing education credits, and access to the certification program. The CEO of the association is worried that AI is changing how their members work day-to-day, and the association's current model of episodic touchpoints — a conference here, a webinar there, a credential renewal every few years — feels increasingly disconnected from what members actually need to navigate.

How should the association rethink how it delivers value to members? What does the next version of this association look like?
